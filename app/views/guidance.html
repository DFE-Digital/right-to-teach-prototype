Guidance

Check a teacher’s record

Check a person's teaching record before they start working in your school or college.

Contents
– Overview
– Check a teacher's record online

Overview

You must check if a candidate for a teaching role is suitable before they start working for you in a school or college in England.

If you use a teacher supply agency you must make sure they’ve checked the candidate’s teaching record.

This guidance tells you:

  • what you can use the Check a teacher’s record service for
  • what you need to access Check a teacher’s record

Check a teacher’s record online

Use the Check a teacher’s record service to check the record of anyone with a teacher reference number (TRN) including:

  • teachers
  • teaching assistants
  • higher learning teaching assistants
  • former teachers

This service helps schools and colleges carry out some of the safeguarding checks that must be completed before staff start work such as:

  • a professional qualifications check
  • a check to see if someone is prohibited from teaching

You can search for a record using someone's last name and date of birth. You do not need to know their TRN, but the service will only show a record if someone has a TRN.

Teaching status and qualifications

Check a teacher’s record will tell you whether someone has:

  • qualified teacher status (QTS) or early years teacher status (EYTS)
  • passed their induction
  • national professional qualifications (NPQ) or mandatory qualifications for teaching pupils with sensory impairments

It will not tell you if someone has qualified teacher learning and skills (QTLS) status. You need to check the Society for Education and Training professional register.

Prohibitions and alerts

Check a teacher’s record will tell you whether someone has:

  • been prohibited from teaching by the Teaching Regulation Agency (TRA)
  • had sanctions or restrictions placed on them by the General Teaching Council for England (GTCE)
  • been found guilty of serious misconduct but not prohibited from teaching

You cannot download and view a full list of people with prohibitions, sanctions or restrictions due to data protection regulations.

Section 128 list

Check a teacher’s record does not provide a list of people who are not allowed to manage or govern schools (called the section 128 list).

You need to check the Section 128 list separately.

Children’s barred list

You cannot use Check a teacher’s record for Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) checks.

Use Check the children’s barred list to carry out a standalone children’s barred list check only if a new employee either:

  • will start working with children while waiting for the result of an enhanced DBS check
  • does not need an enhanced DBS check because they’ve worked with children in a school or college within the last 3 months

Supply agencies cannot use this service as they must carry out an enhanced DBS check with children’s barred list information before employing someone.

'Claiming’ and ‘disowning’ teachers

You cannot add (‘claim’) or remove (‘disown’) a teacher from your organisation in Check a teacher’s record.

This is not required as part of the safeguarding checks you must carry out on staff before they start work.

Accessing Check a teacher’s record

You need a DfE Sign-in account to use Check a teacher’s record. If you do not have one then you can:

  • ask your organisation’s approver to create an account for you
  • use the contact form to request an account if you do not have an approver
  • email teaching.status@education.gov.uk if you work for a teacher supply agency – you should normally receive a response within 5 working days

If you need to check a teacher’s record while you wait for your DfE Sign-in account, email employer.access@education.gov.uk and say ‘urgent query’ in the subject line.

Contact

Email teaching.status@education.gov.uk if you have questions about the new service. We will aim to respond within 5 working days.
